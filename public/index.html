Baku - The City of Winds

Baku, the capital and commercial hub of Azerbaijan, is a low-lying city with coastline along the Caspian Sea. It's famed for its medieval walled old city, which contains the Palace of the Shirvanshahs, a vast royal complex, and the iconic stone Maiden Tower. Karabakh - The Heart of Azerbaijan

Karabakh is a geographic region in southwestern Azerbaijan, extending from the highlands of the Lesser Caucasus down to the lowlands between the Kura and Aras rivers. It is a land of breathtaking mountains, lush valleys, and rich cultural heritage. The city of Shusha, known as the cultural capital of Azerbaijan, is located here, famous for its historical sites and musical traditions.

Quba - The Apple Orchard

Quba is a city in and the capital of the Quba District of Azerbaijan. The city lies on the north-eastern slopes of Shahdag mountain, at an altitude of 600 m above sea level, on the right bank of the Gudial river. Quba is known for its semi-rural atmosphere, lush nature, and especially for its delicious apples. The nearby villages like Khinalig offer a glimpse into ancient Caucasian life and stunning mountain landscapes.

Naftalan - The Healing Oil Resort

Naftalan is a unique health resort, world-famous for its healing crude oil. The Naftalan oil is a special type of oil used for therapeutic baths to treat more than 70 diseases, including musculoskeletal and skin conditions. Thousands of people visit Naftalan's sanatoriums every year to experience this one-of-a-kind natural treatment.

Sheki - The Silk Road Gem

Sheki is one of Azerbaijan's most ancient and picturesque towns, nestled in the forested slopes of the Greater Caucasus Mountains. It was an important stop on the legendary Silk Road. The city is famous for the magnificent Palace of Shaki Khans, with its exquisite stained glass (shebeke) and detailed murals. Sheki is also known for its unique cuisine, particularly its sweet halva.
